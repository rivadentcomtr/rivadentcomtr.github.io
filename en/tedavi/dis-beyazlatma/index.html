Teeth Whitening

Since healthy and white teeth are the symbol of beauty and health; Teeth whitening is a frequently preferred method and one of the most striking points in the society is the beauty of the mouth and teeth, so it is possible to look much more beautiful and healthy thanks to cosmetic applications such as teeth whitening.

Teeth whitening is the process of lightening the color of the teeth, the color of the tooth enamel changes due to genetic or various reasons. As a result, teeth whitening is the chemical change of chromogens inside the tooth.

Teeth whitening can also be defined as a cosmetic application performed as a solution to the problem of internal and external discoloration in the enamel structure, with whitening gels.

Contents

How Is Teeth Whitening (Bleaching) Performed?

First of all, the process starts with tartar cleaning, and the stones on the teeth are completely cleaned with a tooth whitening called polishing.

This is a sensitive procedure and should be done by specialist and professional dentists. The materials accumulated on the tooth surface are cleaned, resulting in a smoother tooth surface.

The soft tissues of the teeth (such as cheek, tongue, lip, gingiva) are then isolated with a barrier. Soft tissues are more sensitive, so they should be protected and kept away from chemicals.

At this stage; it is very important that the whitening chemicals do not come into contact with the gums, if the barrier is not applied successfully, it will also temporarily whiten the gums. However, even if it is temporary; it is useful to be careful, as it will be a very unsettling situation for patients.

After the whitening gel is applied to the teeth, it is activated by a light and 15 – 20 minute sessions are repeated under the control of the doctor until sufficient whiteness is achieved.

After the teeth whitening process, the teeth are cleaned by washing with plenty of water and the sessions are repeated under the control of the doctor until the desired whiteness is achieved, and the procedure is renewed if necessary.

After the bleaching process performed in the clinic, a mold containing whitening gel is placed on the teeth in terms of the protection, continuity and home application of the whitening.

Teeth Whitening (Bleaching) Methods

Teeth whitening process and methods are increasing and developing day by day in parallel with scientific developments.

The whitening process is done in two ways, the first of which is Office Bleaching, which is applied within a few hours in a clinical setting.

The other is the Home Bleaching process called Home Bleaching. However, do not be confused when it comes to home type, you need to place a whitening gel plate, which is performed under the supervision of a doctor and given by him, on your teeth in accordance with the instructions for use told by your dentist, and you need to sleep in that way throughout the night.

It is required to be worn for an average of 1 week and 7 – 8 hours, this procedure is mostly recommended for the permanence and continuity of the process after the bleaching treatment performed in clinics.

You can decide on the most appropriate treatment method by consulting your dentist.

Are There Any Side Effects of Teeth Whitening (Bleaching) Procedure?

Tooth whitening applied by your doctor is a very popular and highly preferred cosmetic dental treatment method, it is not harmful to the teeth when performed by specialist physicians. On the contrary, dental calculus and tooth cleaning is a necessary procedure for teeth.

There is no damage or permanent problem in the teeth, the process targets the colored part of the tooth enamel and has no side effects on the tooth tissue or gums.

There may be a sensitivity to cold – hot food and liquid consumption after whitening, this usually disappears within 48 hours and this sensitivity is considered quite normal by dentists.

There is no side effect of whitening, which is said by physicians, but since the tooth structure will become sensitive for a short time, some details should be paid attention to.

Tea and coffee should not be consumed immediately after bleaching.

It is beneficial to stay away from the consumption of colorful fruits and vegetables, especially red-colored foods.

After whitening, you should pay more attention to your dental care and brush your teeth twice a day, use dental floss and mouthwash to preserve the whiteness for a long time.

Smoking is one of the most harmful habits affecting tooth color, quitting smoking causes a significant lightening in tooth color, reducing cigarette consumption after whitening prevents teeth from recoloring.

Teeth may have a certain color genetically, not always as a result of applications made by dentists.

Why is Teeth Whitening Needed?

It is a fast and reliable procedure that does not have any side effects, and the beauty of teeth draws attention in many places from social life to business life; this is why the dental aesthetics and cosmetics industry exists.

If you haven’t been able to take care of your dental health until now, the color change of your teeth can affect you psychologically, but it is possible for your teeth to regain their former beauty easily with tooth color whitening.

Even if you do not neglect your dental care, your teeth will not look as white as you would like if you smoke and drink a lot of acid, so whitening may be your choice.

The drugs and antibiotics you use as a result of physiological disorders can cause a change in the color of your teeth, as a result of which your teeth will return to their old color; since it will not be easy just by brushing teeth; it is possible to reliably restore your teeth to their original color by whitening.

Undesirable and unpleasant tooth color by the patient; with the control of dentists, it is safely removed with whitening and healthy and beautiful smiles are achieved.

However, in some cases, people may not be suitable for whitening:

  • People with gum disease,
  • If you are addicted to alcohol and cigarettes,
  • To pregnant women and new breastfeeding mothers,
  • For milk teeth,
  • Patients allergic to peroxide,

This procedure cannot be applied to people who have problems with their teeth or cavities; Teeth whitening can be done safely after the appropriate treatment is applied and the problem in the teeth is resolved.

Does Sensitivity Occur After Teeth Bleaching?

More or less sensitivity may be seen after bleaching, but this sensitivity is temporary and disappears within 48 hours. On the first day, with the recommendation of the dentist, painkillers can be used. After teeth whitening, there will be sensitivity even for a short time, so care should be taken until the sensitivity subsides, but the use of whitening toothpaste should be interrupted for a short time because it contains abrasives.

If your teeth are sensitive before whitening, you should inform your dentist and take an action accordingly.

Does Bleaching Damage Tooth Structure?

When teeth whitening is done with the right methods under the control of dentists, it does not cause any harm to the teeth; since the whitening process is very popular and preferred by many people and it is a cosmetic application, it does not cause any harm, does not harm the tooth structure or causes permanent damage.

In addition, it is a useful procedure since gingival problems will be treated and tartar will be cleaned before whitening.

You should prefer centers and clinics that you trust, research and go to, it is possible that whitening that is done unconsciously and not in accordance with the technique can damage your teeth.

On the other hand, an application to be made with natural materials at home causes irreversible damage to the teeth. Food materials such as baking soda and lemon do not whiten the teeth, on the contrary, they both damage the tooth structure and cause the tooth to darken more.

Lemon softens the tooth enamel with its acidic structure, and baking soda erodes the teeth by scratching them; it becomes open to caries and sensitivity and the enamel, which is the strongest structure of the tooth, disappears. Any food material not recommended by physicians should not be used uncontrollably, and teeth whitening should be done under the control of a physician.

How Teeth Lose Their Whiteness (Bleaching)?

It is said that the whitening process lasts between 6 months and 2 years, but since it is a cosmetic procedure, it may differ according to the tooth structure and use.

After the whitening process, the teeth gradually lose their whiteness due to carelessness, neglect of oral and dental care routines, smoking, consuming acidic beverages, ignoring the use of dental floss and mouthwash.

Despite this, it still does not return to its original color, but going to the dentist at certain intervals and repeating the necessary procedures; it is very important for the continuity of whiteness.

When you support office whitening with home bleaching, your teeth will not return to their former state and you will have white teeth for a long time by paying attention to certain criteria.

With a short research, you can reach many people who have done the procedure. Most of those who have done are very satisfied with the transaction, of course, people who leave negative comments will draw your attention.

However, the teeth of the patients who do not comply with some conditions determined by the physicians after bleaching will return to their old color in a short time; for this reason, it is necessary to comply with everything that is said by physicians and to apply it carefully.

Your teeth, which you do not like the color before whitening, will be clean and white after the procedure, the center and the doctor you will choose play a big role in this, choosing a center you trust will ensure you get positive results.

However, a lot of work falls on the person who has the whitening process afterwards; In addition to the satisfaction of the people who have whitening in general, it is possible to achieve healthier teeth by paying more attention to the hygiene and care of the teeth in order to preserve the whiteness.

It has been observed that patients who do not consume alcohol, tea and acidic beverages preserve their teeth whiteness for a longer period of time.

When you see negative comments such as “my teeth have not whitened”, you should definitely consult your doctor and then understand the points to be careful about.

frequently asked questions

Typically, you won't feel pain during dental treatment. Local anesthesia is applied to numb the area before the procedure. After the anesthesia wears off, you might experience mild soreness, but this is temporary.

Teeth whitening is completely safe when done with proper techniques and under the supervision of a qualified dentist. It does not harm your oral health. However, some temporary sensitivity may occur.

Teeth cleaning is very important for preventing gum diseases and maintaining oral health. Tartar buildup can lead to gum inflammation, so regular dental cleanings are recommended.

A dental implant is a titanium post placed in the jawbone to replace a lost tooth. It serves as a stable foundation for a dental crown, providing functionality similar to natural teeth.

The duration of orthodontic treatment varies based on individual needs, but it typically lasts between 1 to 3 years. The treatment time depends on the condition of the teeth and how well you follow the treatment plan.

Children's dental treatment is personalized according to their age and development. Regular check-ups and dental cleanings are essential for maintaining their oral health and preventing future issues.

Orthodontic treatment is usually recommended for children between the ages of 9 and 14, when their growth phase is not complete. However, adults can also undergo orthodontic treatment, and treatment can be effective even after the growth period.

Tooth decay is treated depending on its severity. Superficial cavities are treated with fillings, while deeper cavities may require root canal treatment.

Dental prostheses, such as crowns or bridges, are used to replace missing teeth. They can be recommended for patients with tooth loss to restore both function and aesthetics.

It is recommended to visit the dentist at least twice a year for regular check-ups. Regular visits help with early detection of potential issues and are essential for maintaining optimal oral health.

Gum bleeding is typically a sign of gum inflammation and should not be ignored. You should visit a dentist if you experience gum bleeding to address any potential oral health issues.

The recovery process depends on the type of treatment. For procedures like fillings and cleanings, recovery is usually immediate. However, for treatments like implants or tooth extractions, recovery may take a few days.

Urgent dental treatments can be performed during pregnancy. However, it is generally best to schedule dental procedures during the second trimester (3-6 months). Be sure to discuss anesthesia and medication use with your dentist.

Tooth sensitivity is often caused by enamel erosion or gum recession. Your dentist may recommend treatments to reduce sensitivity, and using sensitivity-reducing toothpaste can also help alleviate discomfort.
